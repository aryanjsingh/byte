NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF) 2.0 
Core Functions: GOVERN, IDENTIFY, PROTECT, DETECT, RESPOND, RECOVER

GOVERN (GV): The organization’s cybersecurity risk management strategy, expectations, and policy are established, communicated, and monitored.
- GV.OC-01: Organizational mission, objectives, and high-level priorities for cybersecurity are understood.

IDENTIFY (ID): The organization’s current cybersecurity risks are understood.
- ID.AM-01: Inventories of hardware, software, services, and data are maintained.
- ID.RA-01: Vulnerabilities in assets are identified, validated, and recorded.

PROTECT (PR): Safeguards to ensure the delivery of critical infrastructure services.
- PR.AA-01: Identities and credentials are managed.
- PR.DS-01: Data-at-rest and data-in-transit are protected.

DETECT (DE): Develop and implement appropriate activities to identify the occurrence of a cybersecurity event.
- DE.CM-01: The network and physical environment are monitored to detect potential cybersecurity events.

RESPOND (RS): Develop and implement appropriate activities to take action regarding a detected cybersecurity incident.
- RS.MA-01: Incidents are contained and mitigated.

RECOVER (RC): Develop and implement appropriate activities to maintain plans for resilience and to restore any capabilities or services that were impaired due to a cybersecurity incident.
- RC.RP-01: Recovery plan is executed during or after an incident.
